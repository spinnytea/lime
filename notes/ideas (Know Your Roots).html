So then, how do I resolve "requirement" dependencies. Because, a house has people, but no all the time.

The concept of a house (for a person) is dependent on it having a kitchen, a place to sleep, and a toilet. It is just as capable of having people inside as it is having a giraffe, but these are not required to fulfill the essence of a house.

Getting all philosophical, the purpose of a house is to "store" the basic necessities of it's owner; for a person, "take in energy" "expel waste" "sleep" "stay warm (not hot and not cold)"

But a house is also supposed to have people inside of it, so it needs to have enough room for the person. Some people also desire belongings, in which case, it needs enough space for this.

The typical locking mechanisms used are a "key lock inside the handle" and/or a "dead bolt"

Sometimes there is an eye-hole, a knocker, or an "inside only" locking mechanism (such as a chain)
